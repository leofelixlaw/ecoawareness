    The Science Behind

    Most

    Ideal Areas

    Clinic & Hospitals

    Germs may transmit and infect easily with high human traffic. A sanitized environment prevents patients, staff, and visitors against risk of infection.

    Gym & Fitness Centres

    During an intensive workout, we usually inhale more air and breathe it more deeply into our lungs. While doing so, we inevitably breathe in more airborne contaminants unknowingly.

    Hotels & Commercial Areas

    Airborne germs are a major risk in high-traffic areas. A sneeze/cough can expel pathogens into the air for an extended duration to trigger respiratory conditions.

    Food & Beverages + Restaurants

    Controlling the level of airborne pollutants such as cooking smells, offensive food odors, and germs are important for an enhanced dining experience.

    Residential homes & Offices

    Indoor air quality can be 2 to 5 times worse than the outdoor air. Often, indoor air contains tiny microbes that can have numerous a nd diverse effects on our health once inhaled into the body.

    Schools & Ecucational Centres

    With children's immune system still developing, providing them a clean and sanitized environment to play and learn in, helps to prevent disease transmission.
